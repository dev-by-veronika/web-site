SEVEN SPECTACULAR PLACES

These are amazing places that nature has created. Scrolling through the gallery, you can see the country and the name of the place - and one day, visit it!

Glowworm Grotto
Waitomo Cave, New Zealand

The only light source is thousands of brilliant blue fireflies that create the illusion of a starry sky

Lake Retba
Senegal, West Africa

The pink color of the lake is created by the harmless Dunaliela Salina bacteria. The depth of the lake does not reach more than 3 meters, where one and a half meters is salty mucus

Antelope Canyon
Arizona, USA

The gorges have been shaped and polished by rainwater flows, the seasonal Antelope Creek river, and grit

Glacial Moraine Lake
Canada

The lake is located at an altitude of 1885 meters above sea level. The shade of turquoise is created by the glaciers that feed the lake

Pamukkale
Denizli Province, Turkey

The water temperature in the tufa reaches 36 degrees. These springs are enriched with a large number of different minerals

Sagano Bamboo Forest
Arashiyama, Kyoto, Japan

The area of the forest covers 16 square km and consists of thousands of huge bamboo plants, up to 76 cm thick and 40 m high

Colored rocks of Zhangye Danxia
China

The predominantly red shade of the mountains is formed from red sandstones and rocks of the Cretaceous period after a dried-up reservoir

THE ENVIRONMENT NEEDS YOUR HELP

“ Nothing affects the state of nature like human activity. Emissions of toxic waste poison water, air and soil and lead to the death of entire ecosystems, deforestation and poaching lead to the extinction of many animal species. But YOU can fix it by following these simple pointer cards:

consume less water

conserve water resources. use a shower instead of a bath. dishwasher instead of hand washing. this way you can save up to 200 liters per day!

consume less electricity

use lamps and table lamps, including the light locally. unplug appliances. hang the wash to dry instead of tumble dry. thus you will save up to 100W per day!

consume less paper

use email instead of paper letters. pay bills online, it will also save your time. buy artificial Christmas trees instead of live trees. this will save a couple of tons of paper or a few hecators of trees a year!

consume less plastic

use cups, food bags and things made from paper or recycled plastic. You can also use reusable fabric bags. also hand over bottles and batteries for recycling and disposal

consume less gasoline

give preference to walking. if possible, replace your car with an electric car. this will reduce exhaust emissions and make the air cleaner

sort the trash

waste sorting will help to manage waste efficiently and reduce the amount of carbon dioxide produced by burning unsorted waste. a lot of garbage can be recycled, while producing things or energy without harming the planet

  For several years I have been interested in studying the internal state of a person, his psycho-emotional background and pronounced psychosomatic disorders on the part of the central nervous system. And I will be glad to share with you a piece of what I managed to find out.

"All diseases are from nerves"

  Our emotions directly affect our health.
  Sometimes neurosis and tension, tachycardia, prolonged headaches and chronic fatigue can be associated with psychosomatics. Psychosomatic disorders are associated with violations of the work of the Central Nervous System.
  There are a few things to help you relax:
- breathing exercises - helps relieve anxiety;
- time in silence and inactivity - time for peace, where you allow yourself to rest physically and mentally;
- the right society - the more positive and joyful people will be around you, the more positive energy you will adopt from them;
- meditation, yoga, massage, physical activity - all this can regulate the work of the nervous system;
- shift the focus - do what you love, read a deep, interesting book or watch a movie;
   ! it is important to remember that first of all, you must clearly understand that your ailments are psychosomatic in nature. Indeed, however, stress is not the cause of the disease, but is only a risk factor and aggravation!
